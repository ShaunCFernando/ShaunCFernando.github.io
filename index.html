Academics & Research

I am a high school junior at Pennsylvania Leadership Charter School, University Scholars Program(USP). By the end of my junior year, I will have completed 16 Advanced Placement (AP) classes. I intend to take three additional AP Classes during my 12th grade year. My GPA is roughly 4.6. I am passionate about physics and calculus. My goal is to pursue a Bachelor of Science degree at the intersection of biomedical and aerospace engineering. I have been conducting DNA, microbial and cancer research since 6th grade. The Walter Reed Research Center supported my early research. During the summer of 7th grade I conducted my research at Central Catholic High School's AP Biology Laboratory under the supervision of Mark Krotec. In 8th grade I worked as an independent researcher at home building my own Arduino-powered robot, "Potamoi" to test the quality of water in the Monangahela, Allegheny and Ohio rivers. Additionally, I built a mobile app, "myH2O" to display near-realtime water quality results. In 9th and 10th grade I conducted research at , Hillman Cancer Center Academy "YES" research program affiliated with University of Pittsburgh Medical Center (UPMC) under the mentorship of Dr. David Boone, the Director of the Department of Biomedical Informatics and CoSBBI. During the two year program I built machine learning (ML) models to predict various cancer biomarkers.

One week in Summer 2025 Keystone Boys State Program

Pennsylvania American Legion Keystone Boys State is a unique week long summertime educational program that does not emphasize classroom lectures and textbook learning. Instead, it focuses on participation and personal experience in a model state, complete with governing bodies and elected public officials. It is designed to mirror the structure and operation of its respective state government. While conducting independent research on the water quality of Pittsburgh’s rivers, I uncovered alarming levels of contamination from industrial waste and other pollutants. I developed a detailed remediation plan, but struggled to engage local and state representatives to share my findings or advocate for meaningful action. This experience revealed a crucial gap in my understanding—not just how to identify and solve scientific problems, but how to navigate the civic systems necessary to implement real change. I am applying to Keystone Boys State to gain firsthand experience in government, collaborate with peers to draft and pass legislation, and build the leadership skills needed to turn research into policy. My long-term goal is to introduce a state-level bill mandating standardized water quality monitoring, transparent public reporting, and community-based remediation programs—beginning with the rivers I’ve already studied. Boys State is the critical next step in transforming my environmental research into civic impact.

Summer 2023, 2022 UPMC Hillman Cancer Institute YES Academy

I applied and was accepted 3 years in a row to a program at the University of Pittsburgh called The Hillman Academy, which was an 8 week full time summer internship program for high school students to gain hands-on experience in various fields (Computational Biology, Bioinformatics, Women's Cancer Research, Surgery, etc.). Accceptance rate for the program was around 15%, with a very rigorous admissions process. The program consisted of a multitude of journal club sessions and research roundtables to facilitate engaging like true scientists by analyzing papers and talking to experts in various areas, but there was a research component as well. Each summer I worked one-on-one with a different mentor in the Department of Biomedical Informatics(CoSBBI), which was the area I was most interested in. With the help of their experience, I created a project in their area of research that we both were interested in exploring, and presented my findings to the rest of the program at the end of the 8 weeks. I would work 9-3 from Monday to Friday for those 8 weeks. As I don't have nearly as much time for research during the school year and focus on traveling for tournaments and training, I am currently not working on it, but I am planning on coordinating with my mentor again this summer to continue our work on implementing LLMs for infectious disease case detection. Later two years I was at Hillman Academy's "YES" program.
